The evidence window for this update is July 19 alone, following the July 18 assessment. A multi-pass review found no newly dated, verifiable frontier-model release, independent long-horizon autonomy result, measured AI-assisted AI R&D acceleration, major compute commitment, or geopolitical action that materially changes alignment with AI 2027. Older announcements republished or discussed today were treated as context, not new evidence.

All five scores therefore hold. The scenario’s decisive early-2026 mechanism remains a roughly 1.5× acceleration of frontier-lab research from AI assistance. Public evidence shows that models are already used inside research loops, but it still does not demonstrate a sustained lab-wide multiplier or reliable automation of open-ended frontier research. METR’s time-horizon work remains the clearest independent framework for testing whether autonomy is lengthening, and no new result in this window resolves that question.

The strongest uncertainty is visibility: private labs may possess substantially better evidence than they publish. The next concrete checkpoint is Moonshot’s promised Kimi K3 weights and technical report by July 27, followed by independent attempts to reproduce its long-horizon engineering claims. Evidence of repeatable multi-day research autonomy or a credible lab-level productivity measurement would move the automation and overall scores; another self-reported benchmark record by itself would not.
